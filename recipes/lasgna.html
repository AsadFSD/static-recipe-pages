Lasagna Recipe

Chef John's handkerchief lasagna is made with paper-thin homemade pasta sheets, folded over a delicate ricotta filling and a light amount of pasta sauce.
The filled "handkerchiefs" are then layered to create one of the best versions of lasagna ever.

Ingredients

Pasta Dough

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour or "00" flour
  • 3 large eggs

Cheese Filling

  • 12 ounces ricotta cheese
  • 1 ounce Parmesan cheese, grated
  • 1 large egg yolk
  • 1/4 cup finely minced Italian parsley
  • 1 teaspoon kosher salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
  • 1 pinch cayenne pepper

Lasagna Assembly

  • 1 cup marinara sauce
  • Grated Parmesan cheese for topping
  • Chopped basil or parsley for garnish

Steps

Step 1

Make the pasta dough by combining flour and eggs. Knead until smooth and let it rest for 30 minutes.

Step 2

Roll out the dough into thin sheets (paper-thin if possible). Cut into large squares or rectangles.

Step 3

Cook pasta sheets in salted boiling water for 30-60 seconds until just tender. Transfer to an ice bath to stop cooking, then drain on a clean towel.

Step 4

Prepare the cheese filling by mixing ricotta, Parmesan, egg yolk, parsley, salt, black pepper, and cayenne in a bowl.

Step 5

Place a spoonful of the cheese filling in the center of each pasta sheet and fold into a handkerchief shape (fold into a triangle or rectangle).

Step 6

Lightly coat a baking dish with marinara sauce. Layer the filled pasta handkerchiefs, adding a small amount of sauce between each layer.

Step 7

Sprinkle with Parmesan cheese on top.

Step 8

Bake in a preheated oven at 375°F (190°C) for about 20-25 minutes or until hot and bubbly.

Step 9

Garnish with chopped basil or parsley and serve hot.
